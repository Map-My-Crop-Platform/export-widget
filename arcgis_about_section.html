FDA Agricultural Monitoring System

AI-Powered Detection and Risk Assessment Platform

The FDA Agricultural Monitoring System is an advanced geospatial intelligence platform developed for the U.S. Food and Drug Administration to enhance food safety oversight through automated detection and classification of agricultural facilities across California, Indiana, and Florida.

This dashboard integrates satellite imagery analysis, deep learning models, and comprehensive geospatial data to support FDA's food safety mission under the Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA).

Mission & Purpose

Supporting FDA Food Safety Objectives

This system directly supports:

  • Produce Safety Rule Compliance (21 CFR Part 112): Identifying potential contamination sources near produce-growing regions
  • Risk-Based Inspection Planning: Prioritizing field investigations based on facility proximity and characteristics
  • Waterborne Pathogen Risk Assessment: Mapping contamination pathways from animal operations to agricultural water systems
  • Preventive Food Safety: Shifting from reactive response to proactive hazard identification

The Problem We're Solving

Traditional agricultural facility monitoring relies on:

AOI I: California - Salinas Valley Region

Model Limitations & Considerations

Technical Limitations

Imagery Constraints

  • Cloud cover may obscure facilities in optical imagery
  • Temporal gaps between imagery updates (2-3 years for NAIP)
  • Seasonal variations in facility appearance
  • Resolution limits for small operations

Classification Challenges

  • Mixed-use facilities may be misclassified
  • Horse operations have lower detection accuracy (75%)
  • Abandoned facilities may be incorrectly included
  • New construction not yet captured in imagery

Geographic Limitations

  • Models trained on specific states may need retraining for new regions
  • Urban areas with dense development create detection challenges
  • Forested areas may obscure smaller facilities

Validation Requirements

FDA Guidance: All AI/ML detections should be validated before regulatory action. High-confidence detections (>90%) have lowest verification priority. Medium and low-confidence detections require field confirmation.

Recommended Validation Protocol

  1. Desktop review using high-resolution imagery and street view
  2. Cross-reference with local/county records
  3. Field verification for medium/low confidence detections
  4. Update model training data with confirmed results

Disclaimer & Data Usage

Data Accuracy Statement

The facility detections displayed in this dashboard are derived from AI/ML models trained on satellite imagery and validated against available ground truth data. While these models achieve high accuracy rates (90%+), all detections should be validated before use in regulatory enforcement actions.
